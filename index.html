How this calculation works

The VA guarantees 25% of any loan above $144,000. The "guaranty" is the dollar amount the VA pledges to cover if the borrower defaults — and the lender requires that 25% guaranty (entitlement + down payment) on every loan. Veterans with full entitlement face no VA loan limit because their guaranty pool is unlimited; veterans with partial entitlement are capped at 25% of the county conforming limit.

Maximum zero-down loan

For partial entitlement: (County limit × 25%) − Used entitlement, divided by 25%. When (entitlement + 25% × purchase price) covers the loan, no down payment is required.

Required down payment

When the purchase price exceeds zero-down capacity, the borrower covers 25% of the gap. Formula: 25% × (price − zero-down max).

Funding fee (effective 2026 — subject to change by VA)

First use: 2.15% (no down), 1.5% (5–9.99% down), 1.25% (10%+ down). Subsequent use: 3.3% / 1.5% / 1.25%. Disabled veterans and qualifying surviving spouses are exempt. Verify current schedule at va.gov before relying on these figures.
